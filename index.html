About

I am an incoming PhD researcher in Computing Science at the University of Glasgow, supervised by Dr Ilyena Hirskyj-Douglas and Professor Mohamed Khamis. I will be based in the Animal-Computer Interaction Lab within the Glasgow Interactive Systems (GIST) research section.

My research centres on animal–computer interaction, with a particular interest in designing multimodal technologies that enable animals to initiate and shape interactions on their own terms. I aim to explore how animal-centred interactive systems can support the agency and wellbeing of companion animals, including cats and dogs, while strengthening relationships between animals and their caregivers.

I also serve as an invited reviewer for journals and conferences in human–computer interaction, including Human–Computer Interaction.
